Problem

People love going to events, but ultimately have to encounter certain logistics as part of their event-going experiences. Venues have rules about what you can and can’t bring inside; and if you don’t know the area, parking can be a difficult task. Simply reaching the venue at all can be time-consuming and finding a place to eat before can be a pain. We sought to find a way to solve these issues for users while also sourcing the necessary information from user contributions.

on a single platform.

Design

ChatterHub, as we would like to call it, is a single community module where users can read about event related queries and contribute to the community.

The whole thing is built on Facebook Comments Plugin.

What are Venue Pages and why is this community module on Venue Page?

StubHub has systematically organized Venue Pages which has listings as per a given venue, such as AT&T Park.

Click here to view AT&T Park's page

Since all of the categories of community discussion are venue specific, (seating, directions, parking, places to eat etc.) it makes sense to put module on venue pages. We thought of putting it on other pages, but here I am dropping another decision tree to show why we put it only on Venue pages and not anywhere else.

Categorization

It was important for us to categorize the comments into different sections such as Venue, Directions, Food and Parking. We integrated four FCPs into one community module by putting them on different tabs.

Why these four?

Based on our user research, competitive analysis and customer support data, we found out that people had most amount of queries for these categories. We even had a field day with the onsite support team to interact with fans firsthand.

Research + Process = Decision making

Primary research through literature study and competitive analysis helped us to frame the problem and find design directions. The design decisions were based on exploration, usability testing in person, UsabilityHub, UserTesting.com, guerilla testing and yes, a lot of quantitative data.

A lot of times it is a business or engineering constraint which pushes you in a certain direction.

The future

We also spent significant amount of our time to think about future and how this community module can grow into something big, maybe a product on it's own. This involved possibilites of having an independent platform like stubhub.com/community or integrating APIs into different tabs which can be used for endorsements.

Version 2.0: Integrating APIs

What if the community module is integrated with useful APIs for venue, food, parking and directions? Could it open more areas for revenue and increase engagement?
