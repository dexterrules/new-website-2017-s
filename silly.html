Seriously Silly

Duration

2 Weeks

Team

Hamid Nadiruzzaman and Me

Tools

Laser cutter, Arduino and craft material

Can we design an experience which helps people overcome their fear by leveraging habituation?

INVOLVEMENT

The Problem

We wanted to create prototype which could capture the essence of facing fear and how people feel and react to the fear. If people are able to face to their fear in a sensible and maybe comfortable environment, they might be able to overcome that. Through this prototype, we wanted to show our participants that there is comfortableness hidden on the other side of the traumatic experience of facing your fear, which also could be viewed as an emotional victory. We wanted to re-articulate problems through play and exploration and provide a safe environment for our participants to interact with our design ideas.

DESIGN

Ideation: Leveraring Habituation

We consider our design idea silly because our prototype is partial, flawed, and integrates silliness. The hand-drawing on the pumpkin is somewhat hard to take seriously. It can be considered as “something children might have made in play-school”. However, we wanted to explore the problem-space for a serious issue. Overcoming one’s fear is a serious problem and we adopted a concept of ‘magic-machine’ to explore the responses for this specific issue.
