MoSM

A museum that showcases the current state of social media along with design fictions that attempt to reveal the consequences social media might have in the future.

My Involvement

The Prompt

As a part of the Experience Design class, we were required to design a museum which captures a certain type of 'experience'. We decided to do one on Social Media.

We thought that social media has been an interesting and innovative movement in the 21st century, and compared to most other movements and events, is very young, but still has captured a lot of history and eventful moments. Social media has fundamentally changed the way people perceive and do things while adding a zest for life. Social media has become an extension of our actual selves and has transformed the way we communicate and socially behave.

The Design

MoSM Selfie Station

Take a group or individual photo at our iconic outdoor photo booth! Before you even enter the museum, you can take and post a photo to any of your social media accounts and remember to use the hashtag #beingatMoSM

Bitmoji Spectacles

Stop by our Bitmoji spectacle vending machine to buy yourself a pair of your own spectacles! These spectacles allow you to view others as their Bitmoji character as you walk through and explore the museum.

Dead Social Media

Throughout the years, there have been many social medias that have short shelf life. Explore the graveyard and remember the fallen mediums that were once a fad.

Gallery of Famous Social Media

Celebrities love social media and have iconic posts. Our exhibit highlights these notorious posts and images from Twitter, Instagram and Snapchat. Don't forget to take a selfie!

Confetti Shower

Have you ever wondered what it's like to celebrate every time you receive a like or a comment on social media? Well now is your chance! Celebrate in our exhibit by simply walking through and showing your #beingatMOSM and let the fun begin!

Place of Peace

Enjoy our place of peace that features fragrant flowers, direct sunlight, a fountain and much more! Just be aware that in order to appreciate it, your cellphone should be put away.

Off Boarding

Have you ever tried quitting social media? It's a heavy decision that can be both disorientating and frustrating. Explore our exhibit and experience the real life off boarding of social media accounts!

process

We started off with doing some research about different phases of using social media and created a journey map out of it.

User interviews

Then, we conducted three user interviews to sort of plot their individual journeys on the journey map that we had created.

Experience Prototyping

Before this we had done an activity on Experience Prototyping, which was an effort to translate digital experience of using social media into something more tangible/physical implementation.

Final exhibit

Then, based on all of the research and prototyping, we created an Exhibit Map, to map out tangible experiences for each of the phases of social media use.

The funnel like shape is to replicate the experience of suffocation. Each room becomes smaller and smaller as you move towards Palace of Peace, making the experience of social media more and more suffocating. This is a symbolic take on our lives and how social media consumes so much of our time, almost suffocating us and leaving no space for other productive activities. This funnel, leads to a Place of Peace where nobody can get a phone network, pushing people to enjoy what nature has to offer. This place reminds people of what they have been missing in life, due to social media, and how they can relish those peaceful moments in nature with the the lack of connectivity. The sudden contrast in funnel like form to an open, courtyard space, will make visitors think about more productive things life has to offer.
