My Involvement

The Problem

Food searching process is time-consuming. People have to go through ratings and reviews on Yelp to make restaurant/food decisions when they are new to a place.Also, humans don't use numbers to give their feedback  of any restaurant experience in a real conversation.

Ideation

UX Flow

Elements

To make Rex behave more human like, there were certain elements which were introduced for the messaging experience on Facebook.

The typing bubble

appears before every card or text Rex sends to the user. This emulates the actual human experience of typing and the information looks less fidgety.

Peer comments

Rex pulls out 'peer comments' from Yelp's database as if it is quoting a mutual friend. It was found in our primary research that people prefer recommendations from friends over reviews from total strangers.

Casual language

Instead of providing more formal sounding buttons like "Ok" or "Proceed" we went for more casual language, with lots of natural language and expression, which is common amongst our target users at Indiana University.

Rationale

Okay. So why a Facebook bot and not simply redesign Yelp's mobile app?

Yelp actually gets way more traffic through mobile and website than their dedicated mobile app

Source: Yelp's factsheet https://www.yelp.com/factsheet The goal of this design was to provide users with quick and easy access to relevant food recommendations without the need to install a new app or use multiple different services or websites. Hence, we decided to use a platform that is nearly ubiquitous on every smartphone; Facebook Messenger. This platform has the obvious benefit of being more mobile friendly than navigating Yelp’s mobile website from a smartphone, but also afforded us the ability to offer photo carousels and other “mini-app” experiences within our conversational UI.

Target users

Students at Indiana University, Bloomington.

Research

To better understand how users make food decisions for online delivery, we conducted a semi-structured user interview on ten of our target users, IU students, who have had experience relying on BTownMenus or Yelp for getting food or making food decisions. We also rolled out a Google form for a quick survey about food delivery.
