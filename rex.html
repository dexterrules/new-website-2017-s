The Problem

Food searching process is time-consuming. People have to go through ratings and reviews on Yelp to make restaurant/food decisions when they are new to a place.Also, humans don't use numbers to give their feedback  of any restaurant experience in a real conversation.

The Design

UX Flow

Elements

To make Rex behave more human like, there were certain elements which were introduced for the messaging experience on Facebook.
The typing bubbleappears before every card or text Rex sends to the user. This emulates the actual human experience of typing and the information looks less fidgety.
Peer comments Rex pulls out 'peer comments' from Yelp's database as if it is quoting a mutual friend. It was found in our primary research that people prefer recommendations from friends over reviews from total strangers.

Cool! 
Casual language Instead of providing more formal sounding buttons like "Ok" or "Proceed" we went for more casual language, with lots of natural language and expression, which is common amongst our target users at Indiana University.
